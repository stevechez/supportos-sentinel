# Onboarding Call Script

For the first live call with a pilot customer. Not a rigid script -- a set
of moments to hit, in order, with the specific talking points that fill the
gaps identified in `docs/product/founder-pilot-walkthrough.md`.

## 1. Before you share your screen

Ask one question first: "What's the specific thing that made you want to
look at this?" Their answer is the buying trigger -- write it down, it's
what you'll come back to on day 30.

## 2. Signup

Let them type, don't type for them -- watching them do it is useful signal
in itself. If they hesitate anywhere, that's real data about the flow, not
just about them.

## 3. The email confirmation wait

This is the one dead-air moment. Say: "While that lands in your inbox --
usually well under a minute -- let me explain what happens next." Use the
gap productively rather than sitting in silence.

## 4. Connecting their source

Say explicitly: **"SupportOS is just what we call the ticketing connection
-- for you that's your Zendesk."** Don't assume they'll infer it. This is
the single most important line in this whole script; skipping it is the
most common way a demo gets confusing.

Let them click Connect themselves if possible -- it's one click, and doing
it themselves is more convincing than watching you do it.

## 5. The first insight

Stop talking when this loads. Let them read it. Then ask: "Does that match
what you'd have guessed, or is that new to you?" Their answer here is the
most important thing you'll learn on the whole call -- write it down.

## 6. The AI brief (optional, don't force it)

If they ask "is this AI?" -- yes, but only for this one button. Say: "Sentinel
found this pattern on its own, using fixed rules, before AI was involved at
all. The AI part just explains it in plainer English if you want that --
everything else you just saw came from straightforward calculation, not a
model guessing."

## 7. Close the call

- [ ] Confirm they know where the Feedback button is.
- [ ] Set an explicit expectation for when you'll follow up (see
      `docs/customer/follow-up-email.md`).
- [ ] Don't leave without them having connected a real source -- an
      unconnected workspace by the time the call ends rarely gets connected
      later.

## If they push back

See `docs/customer/common-objections.md` for the specific responses to the
questions that come up most.
