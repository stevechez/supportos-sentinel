# Customer Interview Guide (Phase 18H)

For pilot check-in conversations. Every question below is written to avoid
leading the customer toward the answer we'd like to hear -- the goal is
finding out what's actually true, including if it's "this isn't valuable."

Ask in this order. Don't explain what Sentinel "is supposed to do" before
asking the Understanding question -- their unprompted answer is the whole
point.

## Understanding

**"What do you think Sentinel does?"**

Don't correct them mid-answer, even if they get it wrong. If their answer
doesn't match what the product actually does, that's not their failure --
it's a finding about the product's clarity, and exactly the kind of thing
that should feed back into marketing copy or onboarding.

Follow-up if useful: "What made you think that?"

## Pain

**"How do you currently identify recurring support problems?"**

Listen for whether they have an existing process at all (a weekly meeting,
a spreadsheet, "we just remember") versus nothing. If they already have a
working process, understand it before assuming Sentinel needs to replace
it -- it might need to fit alongside it instead.

## Value

**"What part of this would save your team the most time?"**

Not "did you like it" -- specifically what would save time, because "I
liked it" and "I would use it" are different things, and only the second one
predicts retention.

Follow-up if useful: "Walk me through the last time you actually needed
that."

## Missing

**"What did you expect to see that was missing?"**

This is the question most likely to surface the team-invite gap identified
in `pilot-experience-audit.md`, or anything else not yet built. Write down
the exact words they use -- "I wanted to see X" is more useful for
prioritization than a paraphrase.

## Buying

**"What would make this worth paying for?"**

Not "would you pay for this" (yes/no questions are easy to answer politely
without meaning it) -- an open question about *what* creates that value
gives an actual criterion to build toward, and reveals whether they're
thinking about it as a real budget line or a nice-to-have.

## After the interview

Log anything concrete as feedback in the product itself (the same
`customer_feedback` table every other feedback entry goes into), tagged with
the right category, so pilot interview findings live in the same place as
in-product feedback rather than a separate document that gets forgotten.
